II. Instructional & Classroom Management

The procedures in this section are designed to support the seamless delivery of courses and the effective management of the learning environment. From establishing course outlines and maintaining office hours to using digital platforms such as Moodle and SIS, these guidelines ensure consistency in instructional standards. This section also details the protocols for room changes and the necessary steps for cancelling or rescheduling classes to minimize disruption to the academic calendar.

Cancelling Classes

Should the need arise to cancel your class, log onto your portal as soon as possible to send an email to your class, and speak to or leave a message for the Chair and Department Administrator. If it is a last-minute cancellation for an 08:45 class, please also leave a message at ext. 2050.

Course Outlines

During the first week of classes, every student must receive a course outline for each course in which they are registered. This can be a hard copy and/or (preferably) a digital copy made available on Moodle, in conjunction with a PowerPoint presentation of the outline during the introductory class. This latter practice saves paper and aligns with the sustainability goals of the department.

The course outline is a contract between the professor and the student. As such, it cannot be subsequently changed without the agreement of the class.
  • 200-level Grading: The department policy of limiting "A" range grades in 200-level courses to a maximum of 25% of the class must be clearly stated.
  • Curving & Partitioning: If you intend to reserve the right to curve final grades, state this explicitly. Likewise, the partitioning of grades (e.g., "20% for the midterm") should be discussed, including the scheme for translating point grades into letter grades.
  • Attendance: If you require attendance, indicate this on the outline and maintain an attendance sign-in sheet.
  • Detail: Your outline should be as comprehensive as possible. Percentage weights and deadlines for assignments, midterms, and papers must be explicitly listed.
  • Deadlines: A copy of your outline must be sent to the Undergraduate Program Assistant one week before the first class.

Undergraduate/Graduate Cross-listed Courses

If you are teaching a 400-level course cross-listed as a graduate 600-level course, you must prepare two separate course outlines. Expectations for graduate students should be higher. Remember that the grading scale for graduate students is different (only A+ through C, and F; no C+, C-, or D range).

Front Page of Course Outlines

The University requires general points of information on the front page. The Chair or Undergraduate Program Assistant will prepare a template and email it to you two weeks before the term begins. Details of your office hours and contact information should also be clearly indicated.

Grade Submissions

Grade Submission via SIS

Grades are submitted electronically via "My Faculty Centre". Your Grade Roster (distinct from the Class Roster) is available through a process identical to the Class Roster. Posting your grades on Moodle only serves to report grades to your students; grades are only official once they are sent to the Chair for approval and posted to the student’s record via My Faculty Centre. When grades are entered, please click “Save” and then change the tab to “Submit for Review“ (this final step sends the grades to the Chair for approval).

Final grades must be submitted no later than seven calendar days (3 calendar days for potential graduating students) after the scheduled final examination. Alternatively, where there is no final examination, grades must be submitted seven calendar days (3 calendar days for potential graduating students) after the last scheduled class. In the event of a take-home exam, grades are due seven calendar days (3 calendar days for potential graduating students) after the last University-scheduled exam. (Note: The acronym used by the University for potential graduating students is “Pot Grads”). Pot Grads will appear on a separate Grade Roster from the rest of the class so that you can process them quickly. Please complete the grading for your Pot Grads first.

III. Academic Evaluation & Grading

This section governs the lifecycle of student assessment, providing a standardized framework for the submission, grading, and retention of academic work. It covers the technical and administrative requirements for processing exams—including multiple-choice coordination—and the specific protocols for grade submissions and subsequent changes. Faculty will also find guidance here on managing specialized notations, such as IP notations for graduate students, and the implementation of course evaluations.

Assignments – Submission, Distribution, & Retention

  • Submission: Students submit work in class, via mailboxes, or electronically (Moodle). The main office does not date-stamp assignments.
  • Returning: Efforts should be made to return work within one week. Leaving assignments in the Reading Room is a last resort and they must be organized alphabetically.
  • Retention: Student work (primarily exams) must be kept for one full academic year (3 terms). Triage outdated materials for shredding once per term.
  • Reading Room (H1254): Readings can be placed on reserve for 2-hour periods. Organize material in discrete folders. Textbooks can also be left on reserve.
  • Models: If requiring models (maquettes), ensure low environmental impact materials are used and stored neatly.

Course Evaluations

  • Full-Time: Evaluated electronically. Results are available via My CU Account.
  • Part-Time: Evaluated via paper questionnaires. Faculty delegate a student to distribute and collect them and must step out of the room. Results are later available online.

Quantitative results are seen by both the faculty member and the Chair; qualitative comments are for the faculty member only.

Exams

During the first week of classes, you must submit a list of courses requiring an exam scheduled by the Exams Office. Specify dates you are unavailable to invigilate and any specific needs (open-book, large space, etc.).

  • Scheduling: No tests or examinations shall take place in class after the 11th week (due to the 12-week requirement).
  • Scripts: Exam scripts must be submitted to the Exams Office by the deadline for free photocopying and transport. If missed, the professor is responsible for the cost and transport.
  • Invigilation: Professors are expected to be present during final exams.
  • Conflicts: Students with three exams back-to-back should contact the Exams Office. Midterm arrangements for individual students are at the professor's discretion.

Grading

An A+ should be reserved for work that is truly outstanding. At the 200 level, no course should have more than 25% of final grades in the "A" range (A+/A/A-). This policy exists to prevent grade inflation. If you reduce grades to lower the number of As, all grades should be lowered by the same amount or according to a fair, transparent principle. The Department Chair may return grades to you for modification if they appear aberrant.

Incomplete (INC) / In Progress (IP) Final Grade

While awarding an INC/IP grade is at the discretion of the professor, accommodations must be made if a student can prove compassionate or extenuating circumstances with supporting documentation.

Academic Re-evaluation (Graduate & Undergraduate)

Students have the right to request an academic re-evaluation if they claim a miscalculation occurred or the evaluation was demonstrably unfair. This is a last resort; students must first attempt to discuss the matter with the professor. If granted, the Chair assigns an alternate professor to re-evaluation the work. The new grade may be higher, lower, or remain the same.

IP Notations for Graduate Students

The In Progress (IP) notation is for students who cannot complete requirements due to extenuating circumstances. It is combined with a grade (e.g., "B/IP"). Work must be completed by the following term's DNE deadline, or the grade becomes permanent. If no grade/IP is assigned within seven days of the exam period, the grade becomes an "F". Consult the Graduate Program Assistant for extensions (IPE).

INC Notations for Undergraduate Students

If a student is allowed to complete work late, assign a grade (missing work = 0) plus the INC notation. Students must apply for Late Completion at the Birks Student Service Centre (fee of $25) by specific deadlines: Feb 1 for Fall; May 15 for Winter/Fall-Winter; and Sept 1 for Summer.

IV. Student Relations & Academic Integrity

This section focuses on maintaining a respectful and ethical academic community and addresses the complexities of the faculty-student relationship. It provides clear procedures for resolving conflicts and upholding the highest standards of academic integrity, including the management of plagiarism and misconduct. Furthermore, this section outlines the specific safety and administrative requirements for students engaged in fieldwork and other experiential learning opportunities.

Faculty-Student Conflict Resolution

Students should first discuss issues with the professor. If unresolved, they can contact the Department Administrator for triage. Conflicts involving sexual harassment should be directed to the Sexual Assault Resource Centre (SARC). Conversations are private, but emails are public documents. Students may also seek advice from the Ombuds Office or Student Advocacy Centre. Private conversations must remain confidential.

Plagiarism and Academic Misconduct

Submit incident reports to the Code Administrator, Dr. Anthony Noce. Professors cannot discuss the incident with the student; they only inform the student that a report was submitted. The Code Administrator will interview the student and decide on sanctions or refer the case to the Academic Hearing Panel (AHP).

Students and Field Work

Professors have an obligation to keep students out of potentially durable situations. Activities involving human subjects (interviews, photos, etc.) must be approved by the Department Ethics Committee. Forms are available from the Service Assistant.

Photos

If photos of human subjects will be published, subjects must give consent via a waiver (available from the Department Administrator).

Class Field Trip Expenditures

Funds are available for excursions. Expenses must be approved by the Chair and processed by the Department Administrator, who will arrange transportation.
