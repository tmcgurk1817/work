Understanding the Internship Process

The MEnv Program's Role

The program and department are here to prepare and support you.

  • Preparation: ENVS 608 seminars provide practical tools like project management, CV/cover letter writing, and interview skills.
  • Resource Provision: We share internship leads, provide access to listings, and facilitate information sharing.
  • Vetting Placements: The coordinator ensures each placement is relevant, meets hour requirements, and provides a safe, respectful work environment.

The Student's Role

You are the driver of your internship search. Your proactivity is key.

  • Finding Opportunities: Actively monitor program postings, check company websites directly, and leverage platforms like LinkedIn.
  • Building Your Network: Connect with peers, faculty, and industry professionals. Attend events and conduct informational interviews.
  • Securing the Placement: You are ultimately responsible for searching for, applying to, and securing your internship.

Your Search Strategy

The Personalized Digital Approach

Move beyond simply filling out online applications. A thoughtful, personalized effort is what will set you apart.

  • Leverage LinkedIn: Build a professional profile, connect with individuals at target companies, and engage with industry conversations.
  • Active Networking: Attend career fairs and industry events. Request informational interviews to learn and uncover hidden opportunities.
  • Targeted Cold Emailing: A well-crafted, personalized email to a recruiter or team lead is powerful and less intrusive than a cold call.

High-Risk, High-Reward Tactics

In-person visits and cold calls can make you stand out, but require thorough preparation and professionalism. They are best used as supplemental tools.

If Visiting In-Person:

  • Best for smaller/medium businesses.
  • Dress professionally.
  • Have your resume and cover letter ready.
  • Be polite, concise, and never pushy.
  • Follow up with a thank-you email.

If Cold Calling:

  • Research who to call and what their team does.
  • Have a clear goal (e.g., get an email address).
  • Script your opening and respect their time.
  • Ask for a better time to call if they are busy.
