Understanding the Internship Process

The MEnv Program's Role

  • Preparation: ENVS 608 seminars provide project management and CV tools.
  • Resource Provision: The Internship Cooordinator shares leads and listing via internal and external networks.
  • Vetting: The coordinator ensures each placement meets program requirements.

The Student's Role

  • Finding Opportunities: Actively monitor program postings and LinkedIn.
  • Building Your Network: Connect with peers and industry professionals.
  • Securing: You are responsible for applying to and landing the internship.

Your Search Strategy

The Personalized Digital Approach

Focus on quality over quantity: optimize LinkedIn, network actively, and send targeted cold emails.

Optimize LinkedIn

  • Use a professional headshot and a headline mentioning "Impact Assessment."
  • Feature your MEnv projects in the "Featured" section.
  • Follow companies from the leads list below to see their news and job posts.
  • Engage with content posted by industry leaders.

Targeted Cold Emailing

  • Research the specific project manager or ESG lead.
  • Subject line should be specific (e.g., "MEnv Student - Inquiry regarding IA projects").
  • Keep it brief: Who you are, why you admire their work, and how you can add value.
  • Attach your CV in PDF format only.

Network Actively

Don't just add people; build relationships. Attend local environmental planning webinars or municipal town halls. Reach out to MEnv alumni for "informational interviews" to learn about their firm’s culture before applying.

High-Risk, High-Reward Tactics

In-person visits and cold calls require professionalism and thorough prep. They are supplemental tools.

If Visiting In-Person:

  • Best for smaller/medium businesses.
  • Dress professionally.
  • Have your resume and cover letter ready.
  • Be polite, concise, and never pushy.
  • Follow up with a thank-you email.

If Cold Calling:

  • Research who to call and their team.
  • Have a clear goal (e.g., get an email address).
  • Script your opening and respect their time.
  • Ask for a better time to call if they are busy.
